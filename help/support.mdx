---
title: "Get Help and Contact the Google Customer Support Team"
sidebarTitle: "Support"
description: "Reach Google support via community forums, email, or live chat. Find response time SLAs, bug reporting, and how to subscribe to status updates."
---

Google offers several ways to get help — from a self-serve community forum for quick answers to a dedicated success manager for Enterprise customers. Choose the channel that best matches your plan and the urgency of your issue.

<Note>
  Before contacting support, check the [Google Status Page](https://status.usegoogle.com) to see whether a known incident may already be affecting your account. Subscribing to incident notifications saves time when platform-wide issues arise.
</Note>

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Community Forum" icon="users" href="https://community.usegoogle.com">
    Browse thousands of threads or post your own question at [community.usegoogle.com](https://community.usegoogle.com). The community is monitored by Google staff and knowledgeable power users who can often answer questions faster than formal support channels.

    Best for: how-to questions, sharing tips, feature discussions, and finding answers to common problems without waiting for a support agent.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Email Support" icon="envelope" href="mailto:support@google.com">
    Send a detailed description of your issue to [support@google.com](mailto:support@google.com). Include your workspace ID, the steps to reproduce the problem, and any relevant error messages or screenshots.

    **Response time SLAs by plan:**

    | Plan | First response |
    |---|---|
    | Free | 5 business days |
    | Pro | 2 business days |
    | Enterprise | 4 hours |
  </Card>

  <Card title="Live Chat" icon="message" href="https://usegoogle.com/support/chat">
    Real-time chat with a Google support agent is available to **Pro** and **Enterprise** customers. Start a session by clicking the **?** help icon in the bottom-right corner of any Google dashboard page.

    Live chat is available Monday–Friday, 09:00–18:00 UTC. Outside of these hours, messages are converted to email tickets and answered within your plan's SLA.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Bug Reports & Feature Requests" icon="bug" href="https://usegoogle.com/feedback">
    Found a bug or have an idea for a new feature? Submit it via the [feedback form](https://usegoogle.com/feedback). Your report goes directly to the Google product team.

    - **Bug reports** — include reproduction steps and expected vs. actual behaviour.
    - **Feature requests** — describe your use case so the team can understand the problem you're solving.

    You'll receive an automated confirmation with a tracking ID; the team follows up if more information is needed.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Status Page" icon="signal" href="https://status.usegoogle.com">
    The [Google Status Page](https://status.usegoogle.com) shows real-time and historical uptime for all platform components. To subscribe to incident notifications:

    1. Open [status.usegoogle.com](https://status.usegoogle.com).
    2. Click **Subscribe to updates** in the top-right corner.
    3. Choose your preferred channel — email, SMS, or webhook.
    4. Select the components you want to monitor and confirm your subscription.

    You'll receive alerts as soon as an incident is opened, updated, or resolved.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Enterprise Success Manager" icon="star">
    Every Enterprise plan includes a **dedicated Customer Success Manager (CSM)** who serves as your primary point of contact at Google. Your CSM helps you with:

    - Onboarding and initial workspace configuration
    - Quarterly business reviews and usage optimisation
    - Escalating critical issues directly to the engineering team
    - Early access to new features and beta programmes

    Contact your assigned CSM directly via the private Slack channel set up during onboarding, or reach out to [support@google.com](mailto:support@google.com) to be connected if you haven't yet been assigned one.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>
